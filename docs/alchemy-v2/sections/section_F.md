# Segment F — 1:36–2:00

Frame source: 2 fps PNGs, 640x360, `/tmp/alchemy_frames/f_NNNN.png`. This segment covers `f_0193` (t=96.0s) through `f_0241` (t=120.0s). Time for a frame = (NNNN-1)*0.5.

This segment is the richest, most varied stretch in the whole video. It contains FOUR clearly distinguishable scenes plus two transitions:

1. **Scene F1 — Fluid Topographic "Glowing Ring" continuation** (96.0s → ~102.0s, f_0193–f_0205): the carry-over of the prior segment's liquid neon background with two Orbiters trailing a vertical jagged waveform line, hue-cycling purple → green → teal → blue.
2. **Scene F2 — Fluid "Anemone / Wireframe Net" bloom** (~102.0s → ~107.5s, f_0206–f_0216): the fluid bg blooms into radiating filament nets (green/red "hair") over a swirling lava-lamp wash, ending in a hard diagonal mirror-fold that announces the stripe scene.
3. **Scene F3 — Vertical Bars & Symmetry (green/black moiré stripes + oscilloscope band)** (~108.0s → ~116.0s, f_0217–f_0232): the signature literal scene — vertical green/black panning stripes with full quad mirror symmetry, an X-shaped diagonal crossing pair of waveform lines, a central horizontal **oscilloscope band** that turns red then green, a pulsing central diamond wireframe, a Starburst/Anemone cluster, and ringed Orbiter dots in the corners.
4. **Scene F4 — 3D Ribbon Plane + hovering Anemone** (~116.5s → 120.0s, f_0233–f_0241): the camera drops into a true 3D perspective; a wide iridescent rainbow **ribbon/plane** sweeps diagonally across a near-black space, an Orbiter pair re-forms with its connecting waveform line, and a fuzzy Starburst/Anemone "dandelion" sits on the ribbon and blooms from gold → blue.

Two transitions are **morphs via frame-feedback warp**, not hard cuts, EXCEPT one true diagonal mirror-fold (f_0216) and the dissolve into 3D (f_0232→f_0233) which reads as a fast morph through black. Details per scene below.

## Scene F1 — Fluid Topographic "Glowing Ring" continuation (96.0s–~102.0s, f_0193–f_0205)

**Background.** A full-frame fluid/topographic neon field — the classic Alchemy "oil on water" look. Broad smeared bands of color flow outward from a soft central depression (a darker "pupil" / well in the middle, ringed by a lighter rim — the "Glowing Ring"). The texture is heavily motion-blurred with long radial streaks, as if the whole field is being continuously zoomed-out and warped (strong frame-feedback). Across f_0193→f_0205 the dominant hue cycles smoothly: f_0193 muddy purple/olive, f_0194–f_0195 magenta+lime-green clash, f_0196–f_0197 green-dominant over purple, f_0198–f_0201 saturated grass-green (a near-monochrome green "anemone" peak at f_0199–f_0200), f_0202–f_0204 green→teal→cyan, f_0205 settles into teal/cyan with a hot red-orange diagonal streak through center. This is the slow ~15–30s hue drift noted in the project memory — confirm over the whole span, not one frame.

**Foreground elements & counts.** TWO Orbiters (the WMP signature) plus a connecting waveform line:
- f_0193–f_0196: a single near-vertical **jagged waveform line** runs top→bottom through the center, faint white/green, with small bright bead-clusters (the Orbiters) at its top and bottom ends. At f_0194 the two Orbiters are clearly visible as small teal/green ringed dots, upper-left and lower-right of center.
- f_0197: the waveform line is now a bold lime-green vertical zig-zag, thick and luminous, dead center — this is a real audio waveform drawn vertically (dense small jaggedness = live samples), not a synthetic sine.
- f_0205: the two Orbiters reappear as **yellow glowing dots** riding a hot red/orange smeared "comet" streak that crosses the frame diagonally (lower-left to upper-right), each dot trailing a short jagged tail.

**Geometry / symmetry.** No mirror symmetry yet — this is free-form fluid. Loose radial symmetry about the central well only. The waveform line is the one hard linear element over an otherwise organic field.

**Motion.** Continuous outward radial flow + slow swirl; the central well slowly drifts and breathes. Orbiters drift slowly along their connecting line. Heavy motion blur on everything.

**Camera/perspective.** 2D, looking straight down onto the fluid surface (top-down "lava lamp" / "weather map" framing). No 3D depth cues.

**Motion blur / feedback (decay).** Very high. Long radial smears imply `decay ≈ 0.96–0.98` plus an outward `zoom > 1` each frame and a slow `rot`. The whole field looks like it is being continuously dragged outward and rotated.

**Waveforms.** The vertical jagged line IS the audio waveform (time-domain). Rendered as a single bright poly-line down the screen center, displaced left/right by sample amplitude.

**Color scheme & hue cycling.** Full slow rainbow drift across ~6s here (purple→green→teal→cyan). Saturated but the project's "keep it muted" rule applies — these are dusty, not neon; greens are grass/sage, not electric.

**Audio-reactivity hypotheses.** Orbiter brightness/size pulses on bass; the waveform line's left/right jaggedness is direct time-domain amplitude; the green "anemone peak" at f_0199–f_0200 likely a loud passage (whole field flares one hue on a bass hit).

## Scene F2 — Fluid "Anemone / Wireframe Net" bloom (~102.0s–~107.5s, f_0206–f_0216)

**Transition in:** morph (no cut). f_0205→f_0206 the fluid continues but the Orbiters at f_0206 become two crisp **ringed concentric-circle "targets"** (bullseye sprites, yellow rings) sitting on a magenta/yellow comet streak — same fluid bg, just sharper foreground sprites.

**Background.** Same fluid neon field, now teal/green-dominant with red and magenta veins. The central well is still present but increasingly overgrown by filaments. By f_0209 the field has bloomed into a dense **Wireframe Net / Anemone**: hundreds of thin curved lime-green hair-like lines radiating and arcing from the center over a dark purple/blue swirl (f_0209, f_0211, f_0213). f_0212 adds **red root-like filaments** branching from the corners inward (red lightning/veins) over a green-and-yellow fluid — strong green/red complementary clash. f_0214 returns to pale white hair-strands over green.

**Foreground elements & counts.** Two ringed Orbiters persist (f_0206 bullseyes; f_0215 two magenta-cored ringed dots at top-right and bottom). The dominant foreground is the radiating filament net — the "hair" is real-waveform-driven (dense fine jaggedness). f_0210 is a near-empty breath: just a dark central lens with two tiny magenta ringed Orbiter dots in opposite corners (top-right, bottom-center) and faint horizontal scan-lines beginning to appear — the first hint of the stripe scene.

**Geometry / symmetry.** Still radial/organic. NO quad mirror yet — until the very last frame of the scene.

**Transition out (the mirror-fold):** **f_0216 is a hard diagonal mirror-fold.** The frame splits along a top-left↔bottom-right diagonal: upper-left triangle shows a tan/olive flat plane with a single red-cored ringed Orbiter at bottom; the rest shows green with horizontal red ripples and a second ringed Orbiter top-right. This is the visual "hinge" — the engine has switched on kaleidoscopic mirroring. It reads as an abrupt fold, the announcement of Scene F3. Treat ~107.5s as the F2→F3 boundary.

**Motion.** Filaments sway and breathe outward; net density pulses with audio. Orbiters drift. Heavy feedback smear continues.

**Camera/perspective.** Still 2D top-down.

**Feedback (decay).** High (`~0.95`); the hair-strands leave faint trails.

**Color.** Green-dominant with red/magenta accents, muted. Complementary green/red is the signature of this sub-scene.

**Audio-reactivity.** Filament count/length and net "explosion" track treble; red veins flare on transients; Orbiter rings pulse on bass.

## Scene F3 — Vertical Bars & Symmetry: moiré stripes + oscilloscope band (~108.0s–~116.0s, f_0217–f_0232)

This is the literal centerpiece of the segment. Describe carefully.

**Background — the vertical green/black panning stripes (moiré).** The whole frame fills with **vertical bars**: alternating bright (green/olive/red/magenta-tinted) and near-black columns, running top to bottom, roughly 18–28 visible columns across the 640px width (column pitch ≈ 12–18px, varying). The stripes are NOT static: they **pan/scroll horizontally** and their pitch breathes, so adjacent passes interfere and produce a shimmering **moiré**. Because the scene is quad-mirrored (see below), the left and right halves are mirror images, so the stripes appear to converge/pinch at the vertical centerline. The bar COLOR cycles over the scene: f_0217–f_0223 green/olive with dark-red columns (muddy forest palette), f_0224 magenta+green columns, f_0225–f_0227 olive-green + purple/magenta columns, f_0228–f_0232 vivid lime-green + dark columns with red bleeding in at center. Each bar is soft-edged (gaussian), not hard rectangles — they look like backlit venetian blinds.

**Symmetry.** FULL quad mirror — left/right AND top/bottom are mirrored about the frame center (f_0217–f_0223 clearly show top half = flipped bottom half). The result is a strong "X" feel.

**The central horizontal oscilloscope band.** Through the vertical centerline (y=0.5) runs a bright **horizontal waveform/oscilloscope band**. f_0228 shows it as a continuous **magenta horizontal bar** with regular vertical notches (a blocky waveform readout). f_0229–f_0230 it becomes a thick **red horizontal band** spanning the full width, again notched like a level meter. f_0231–f_0232 it turns **green/lime** and clearly shows a centered amplitude bulge — louder samples in the middle, tapering to the edges. This is the literal oscilloscope: one horizontal waveform strip, mirror-doubled (the top-bottom mirror makes it read as two waveforms meeting at center, like a classic mirrored VU). Color of the band cycles magenta→red→green.

**The "X" — diagonal waveform-line crossing pair.** Two long thin lines cross the frame as a giant **X** (top-left↔bottom-right and top-right↔bottom-left), intersecting dead center. f_0220–f_0223 they are faint white wireframe diagonals forming a central **diamond/lozenge** (the "central diamond wireframe pulsing"). f_0229–f_0233 the X-lines become bright and jagged — they are **real waveform lines** (green/cyan/magenta, finely zig-zagged along their length, f_0232 especially). The Orbiters ride the ends of these diagonals: small ringed dots in all four corners (f_0220 four ringed dots; f_0229 magenta ringed dots top-right & sides; f_0231 white-ringed dots in corners).

**Central diamond wireframe + Starburst/Anemone.** At the X's crossing sits a pulsing cluster: f_0220–f_0222 a thin white diamond/lozenge wireframe outline; f_0226 a **magenta Starburst/Anemone** (fine radiating filament puff) on the central band; f_0227 a **lime-green Starburst** puff. These pulse and bloom on the beat over the central oscilloscope band.

**Geometry summary.** Vertical stripes (bg) + horizontal oscilloscope band (mid) + diagonal X waveform lines (fg) + central diamond/anemone (focal) + 4 corner Orbiters. Everything quad-mirrored.

**Motion.** Stripes pan horizontally and their pitch breathes (the moiré shimmer). The X-lines rotate/scissor slightly and their jaggedness animates with audio. The central anemone pulses. f_0223 briefly breaks the vertical stripes into a **horizontal-bar + diagonal-wedge kaleidoscope** (a 4-fold pinwheel) before snapping back to vertical bars at f_0224 — a momentary symmetry-mode flip.

**Camera/perspective.** 2D and flat, but the quad mirror + converging stripes give a faux-tunnel/center-pinch illusion. No real 3D yet.

**Motion blur / feedback.** Lower than F1/F2 — stripes are crisp, so `decay` is lower here (`~0.9–0.92`) or the bars are drawn fresh each frame over a darker buffer. Some horizontal smear on the panning.

**Waveforms.** TWO waveform renderings simultaneously: (a) the central horizontal oscilloscope band (time-domain amplitude as a notched bar, mirror-doubled top/bottom), and (b) the diagonal X-lines (time-domain jaggedness along each diagonal). Both are direct audio amplitude.

**Color scheme & hue cycling.** Green/black base, with the bars and band cycling green↔magenta↔red over the ~8s scene. Muted forest-green dominant; the red/magenta band is the brightest element.

**Audio-reactivity hypotheses.** Stripe pan speed and pitch (the moiré) likely driven by bass/tempo; the central oscilloscope band height/notching is direct time-domain amplitude; the diagonal X-lines' jaggedness is direct waveform; the central anemone blooms on transients; corner Orbiters pulse on bass.

**Transition out:** **f_0232→f_0233 is a fast morph through near-black into 3D.** f_0232 still has stripes + green oscilloscope + X-lines; f_0233 the stripes are crushed into the upper-left as a small receding patch and a 3D ground-plane with perspective scan-lines fills the lower frame — the engine has tilted the camera. Reads as a quick warp/zoom-through, ~116.0–116.5s.

**Foreground — the iridescent ribbon/plane.** A single wide **flat ribbon (a tilted plane/band)** sweeps diagonally across the frame, lower-left to upper-right, in true perspective (wider/nearer at one end). **Orbiters + connecting waveform line re-form.** f_0237–f_0238: the two Orbiters return as **green/magenta ringed glowing dots** at the ends of a second diagonal, joined by a fine **jagged waveform line** that crosses the ribbon — the classic "two circles joined by a waveform line" WMP signature, now in 3D over the ribbon. One Orbiter top-right, one bottom-left.

**Starburst/Anemone "dandelion."** f_0238–f_0241: a fuzzy spherical **Anemone/dandelion puff** sits ON the ribbon at center and blooms outward, cycling color: f_0238–f_0239 gold/tan fuzzy ball with fine radiating filaments; f_0240 white/blue puff; f_0241 a fully bloomed **cyan/teal dandelion** (radiating spokes from a dark center, like a sea-urchin or blown dandelion). **Geometry / symmetry.** No mirror symmetry — single asymmetric 3D composition (ribbon + one anemone + Orbiter pair). The only repetition is the ribbon's own width gradient.

**Motion.** The ribbon slowly rotates/tilts in 3D (the camera or the plane swings). The anemone blooms and rotates. Orbiters drift along their diagonal.

**Camera/perspective.** TRUE 3D — perspective foreshortening on the ground-plane scan-lines and on the ribbon. This is the "3D Ribbon plane begins to emerge" called out in the brief: confirmed, it fully emerges by f_0235 and dominates through f_0241.

**Motion blur / feedback.** Low-to-moderate; the ribbon is crisp with a soft bloom/glow around it. Dark background = low decay over black, but bright elements have a soft additive halo (bloom).

**Color scheme & hue cycling.** Dark (near-black) field with a single full-rainbow iridescent ribbon and a color-cycling anemone (gold→white→cyan). High contrast: muted-dark bg, brighter-but-still-pastel ribbon. The rainbow is smooth/pastel, not neon — keep it soft per the muting rule.

**Audio-reactivity hypotheses.** Anemone bloom size = treble/transients; ribbon brightness/hot-spot = bass; the waveform line joining the Orbiters is direct time-domain; ribbon tilt may be a slow time-driven rotation independent of audio.

## Implementation direction for a Butterchurn author

**Coordinate system.** Butterchurn `point_eqs`/shaders use 0..1 with **0.5 = center**; `frame_eqs` audio on `t` (`t.bass/mid/treb` + `_att`). Aspect-correct any radial math with `d.x *= resolution.x/resolution.y`.

**Scene F1/F2 (fluid bg + Orbiters + waveform line).** Reuse the *Dance of the Freaky Circles* pattern verbatim: two `circleWave` Orbiters orbiting + a `waveLine()` point_eqs marching A→B with perpendicular displacement from `a.value1/value2`. Background: an fbm/`pal`-driven warp shader with strong outward `zoom (1.01–1.03)` + small `rot` + `decay 0.96` for the radial smear. Hue-cycle with `pal(time*0.03)` (slow ~20s drift). For F2's filament net, raise the wave count (many faint `circleWave`/`waveLine` shapes) and drive their reach off `t.treb_att`. Keep colors muted (desaturate the palette, Reinhard tone-map the comp).

**Scene F3 — moiré stripes (comp shader).** Render vertical bars in the **comp** shader, NOT geometry. Body sketch (locals must avoid reserved `ang`/`rad`):
```
vec2 p = uv; p.x = abs(p.x-0.5)+0.5;            // left/right mirror
p.y = abs(p.y-0.5)+0.5;                          // top/bottom mirror (quad)
float pan = time*0.15 + bass*0.4;                // horizontal pan + bass kick
float pitch = 22.0 + 6.0*sin(time*0.2);          // breathing column count
float bars = 0.5+0.5*cos((p.x*pitch + pan)*6.2831);
bars = pow(bars, 3.0);                            // soft venetian-blind edges
vec3 col = mix(vec3(0.03,0.05,0.03), pal(time*0.05), bars);  // green/black, cycling
```
The two overlapping mirrored passes give the moiré for free. Keep `decay ~0.9` (crisp). 

**Scene F3 — oscilloscope band (custom waves, time-domain).** Add a horizontal `waveLine()` across y=0.5 whose `a.y = 0.5 + a.value1 * scale` (or render as a filled notched bar by setting two stacked waves at 0.5±value). Mirror top/bottom by drawing a second wave at `0.5 - a.value1*scale`. Cycle band color magenta→red→green with a `sin(time*0.1)` mix. The diagonal X = two more `waveLine` shapes oriented along the diagonals, displacement from `a.value2`. Central diamond = a thin `waveLine` quad outline pulsing on `t.bass`; central Starburst = a dense radial `circleWave` blooming on `t.treb_att`.

**Scene F4 — 3D ribbon.** Fake 3D in the comp shader with a perspective remap: `vec2 q = (uv-0.5); q /= (0.5 + q.y*tilt);` then draw a soft horizontal band `smoothstep` around a tilted line, colored by a rainbow `pal(q.x + time*0.1)`, tone-mapped soft. Ground scan-lines: `fract(q.y*N)` thresholded, fading with distance. The hovering anemone = a `circleWave` dandelion (many radial spokes, `a.r` displaced by `a.value1`) blooming on treble, hue-cycling gold→cyan. Orbiter pair + joining `waveLine` as in Dance, positioned over the ribbon. Background near-black with additive bloom on the bright elements (no heavy feedback).

**Transitions.** F1→F2→F3 are feedback morphs (don't cut — crossfade the comp scene mix over ~1s). F2→F3 mirror-fold: ramp the quad-mirror `abs()` folds in over ~0.5s. F3→F4: quick `zoom`-through to black then fade the 3D scene in (~0.5s).
