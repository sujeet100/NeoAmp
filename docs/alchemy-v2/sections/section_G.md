# Segment G — 2:00–2:28

Frame source: 2 fps PNGs, 640×360, `/tmp/alchemy_frames/`. Segment covers
`f_0241` (t=120.0s) through `f_0297` (t=148.0s). Time = (NNNN−1)×0.5.
Frame→time crib: f_0241=120.0, f_0245=122.0, f_0251=125.0, f_0257=128.0,
f_0265=132.0, f_0271=135.0, f_0277=138.0, f_0283=141.0, f_0289=144.0,
f_0293=146.0, f_0297=148.0.

This segment is a single continuous "Alchemy" act built almost entirely from the
two recurring motifs of this visualizer: the **Orbiters** (a pair of glowing
magenta/violet disc-points that orbit and drag long jagged live-audio waveform
trails) and a **central pulsing cluster** that mutates through three forms — a
fuzzy feathery **Anemone/Starburst**, a flat undulating **3D Ribbon plane**, and a
straight **radiating Wireframe-Net fan**. The whole segment is dominated by very
heavy directional frame-feedback that smears every bright element into long
diagonal rainbow streaks. There are NO hard cuts in this 28-second window — every
scene change is a continuous morph/camera move. I identify four overlapping
phases, each documented as its own scene below, with the morph timestamps where
one form dissolves into the next.

Global palette across the whole segment: near-black background with a faint
**dark maroon / oxblood haze** (not pure black — there is a low-saturation deep-red
wash, strongest top-center, e.g. f_0241, f_0245, f_0287). Foreground hue is a
**magenta ↔ green** duotone that the feedback smears into a full secondary rainbow
(red/blue/cyan/yellow ride along inside the trails). Magenta and green are the two
"pure" hues; everything else is feedback intermodulation. This magenta/green
duality is the strongest signature of the segment and must be preserved.

## Scene G1 — Anemone over the Ribbon, Orbiters launch (t≈120.0–124.0, f_0241–f_0249)

**Background.** Black with the dark-maroon top-center haze. Bottom third of the
frame holds a low, near-horizontal **rainbow ribbon/plane** — a soft horizontal
band of smeared pastel color (gold/teal/violet) running left-to-right across the
bottom quarter (clearest in f_0241, f_0243). It reads as a glowing horizontal
surface seen nearly edge-on, with the camera just above it. There is a tiny green
speck of leftover feedback on the right edge (f_0241, f_0242).

**Foreground — the Anemone/Starburst.** Center of frame (≈center-x, slightly above
mid-height) sits a **fuzzy feathery radial cluster** — the Anemone. In f_0241 it is
**cyan/teal** with fine hair-like filaments radiating from a darker center
(a classic dandelion/sea-urchin puff, ~120 px wide). By f_0242 it has rotated/
recolored to **green on top, blue underneath**; by f_0243 it is fully **green** and
larger (~180 px). The filaments are short, soft, slightly curved — drawn as many
fine lines from a common center with additive glow, NOT straight rays. It sits on
the ribbon and casts a soft blue pool of light onto it (f_0241, f_0242). The center
is a dark void (the eye of the anemone).

**Orbiters launch.** Beginning f_0243 (t=121.0) two **magenta-violet disc points**
appear at opposite diagonal extremes — one upper-right, one lower-left — connected
by a single thin violet line that passes through the anemone. This is the Orbiter
pair forming. The connecting line is at this moment fairly straight (low audio).
By f_0245 (t=122.0) the line has erupted into a **dense jagged zigzag** — a real
audio oscilloscope waveform — running the full upper-right→lower-left diagonal,
with the two magenta discs at its endpoints (disc upper-right ≈(430,40),
disc lower-left ≈(150,330)). The zigzag has the dense, irregular tooth-spacing of
a live waveform, not a regular triangle wave. A faint thin green centerline runs
alongside it (the "spine" the waveform is displaced from).

**Motion / camera.** The anemone pulses and rotates slowly in place. The Orbiters
swing: between f_0243 and f_0249 the diagonal connecting waveform rotates clockwise
and flattens toward horizontal, and the whole thing begins shedding into trails.

**Feedback.** Heavy directional feedback: each waveform position is smeared down-
and-rightward, producing stacked ghost copies (f_0247–f_0249 show the zigzag
multiplied into a thick combed band of parallel waveform ghosts — like a venetian-
blind / comb texture, white/grey teeth over colored fill). This is the visual
fingerprint of strong frame-feedback applied to a moving high-frequency line.

**Audio-reactivity hypotheses.** Anemone diameter ∝ overall energy (bass+mid);
filament length pulses on beats. Orbiter waveform displacement amplitude = live
oscilloscope (the jaggedness IS the time-domain samples). Orbiter orbital speed
appears beat-locked (the diagonal swings on the beat). Color flip cyan→green→
magenta tracks the slow hue cycle, not audio.

## Scene G2 — The Ribbon takes over, Orbiters weave above/below (t≈124.5–131.5, f_0250–f_0263)

**This is the canonical "3D Ribbon + Orbiters" scene the brief predicted.**

**Background.** Black with maroon haze, the haze now spread lower and dimmer. The
discrete ribbon-at-bottom of G1 has risen and tilted: the entire composition is now
organized around a single **diagonal ribbon plane** running lower-left→upper-right
across the whole frame (f_0257, f_0259), later flattening toward horizontal
(f_0263). The ribbon is a **thick glowing horizontal-ish band** of smeared rainbow
(magenta/green/gold/teal stacked in horizontal feedback striations) — exactly the
"thick glowing plane undulating slowly with a fuzzy flare on top" described in the
brief. Confirmed.

**The ribbon shape.** It is not flat — it has a raised **central hump/peak**
(clearest f_0255, f_0257, f_0259): a soft mound rising off the ribbon mid-span,
topped by a wispy white/green feathery flare (the anemone has melted down onto the
ribbon and become the flare on its crest). f_0259 shows this best: a green-and-
white tangled tuft sitting on the apex of the diagonal ribbon, with the ribbon's
rainbow body sweeping down to both lower-left and upper-right corners. The ribbon
undulates slowly — the hump migrates and the whole plane rocks (f_0261 the apex has
shifted right and the tuft is bluer/violet; f_0263 the ribbon has flattened toward
horizontal and the tuft is a white spidery wisp on top).

**Orbiters.** The two magenta discs ride at the **two far ends of the ribbon**
(upper-right + lower-left in f_0255: disc top-right ≈(560,75), disc bottom-left
≈(60,295)). Each drags a **jagged live-audio waveform trail** that runs along /
just above the ribbon surface — the waveform is the bright thin gold/pink jagged
line tracing the ribbon's top edge (f_0255, f_0257), zig-zagging densely. The
waveform weaves **above and below** the ribbon's centerline as it marches end to
end — the displacement is perpendicular to the ribbon axis, driven by live samples.
By f_0259–f_0261 the Orbiter discs themselves fade into the feedback (their trails
remain as the dominant jagged gold/green/violet zigzag lines crossing the frame).

**Motion / camera.** Slow. The ribbon rocks and the apex drifts; the camera holds
roughly level, looking slightly down the ribbon. Over f_0255→f_0263 the dominant
diagonal slowly rotates from steep (lower-left→upper-right ~35°) toward shallow
(~15°, nearly horizontal). Everything drifts down-left as feedback pulls it.

**Feedback (heavy, directional).** This is the strongest-feedback scene. Every
bright element leaves a long **diagonal rainbow streak** parallel to the ribbon
axis. The waveform's teeth smear into the characteristic combed/striped texture.
Characterize for implementation as: high `decay` (~0.96–0.98) + a directional
`dx/dy` push along the ribbon axis (down-left) + slight `zoom` <1 so trails also
pull toward a vanishing point. The result is the long energetic diagonal streaks
in magenta/green the brief flagged.

**Color.** Magenta/green duotone fully developed; feedback creates the rainbow
inside the streaks (red/blue/cyan/gold). The hump flare cycles teal→green→white→
violet over the scene.

**Audio hypotheses.** Ribbon vertical undulation = bass envelope (slow). Waveform
jaggedness along the top = treble/mid time-domain. Orbiter end positions oscillate
on the beat. Flare brightness on the crest spikes with bass attack.

## Scene G3 — Wireframe-Net tunnel, Orbiters orbit the vanishing point (t≈132.0–139.0, f_0264–f_0279)

**Morph in at t≈132.0 (f_0264/f_0265).** The ribbon's feedback striations
**reorganize from horizontal smears into straight radial lines converging on a
center vanishing point** — the scene becomes a **Wireframe Net / radiating fan**.
f_0264 catches the morph mid-way (the comb texture is bending into a fan converging
center-left). f_0265 (t=132.0) is fully a **starburst of thin straight green/teal
lines** radiating from a central point, like looking down a wire tunnel or at a
giant pinwheel of taut threads. This is a continuous morph, not a cut.

**Geometry.** Hundreds of fine straight radial lines emanate from a center that
sits roughly mid-frame and drifts (center-left in f_0265, center in f_0271,
center-right in f_0273). The lines are evenly angularly spaced → reads as a 3D
**funnel/tunnel** or a spoked wheel seen in perspective (f_0271, f_0273, f_0275
have a clear dark elliptical "throat" at the center → a tunnel mouth the camera
looks into). The net color is desaturated green/grey/teal threads.

**Orbiters.** They are now explicit and prominent again: two **magenta/red ringed
disc points** (a bright core inside a red halo ring — f_0271, f_0273, f_0275 show
the ring clearly) orbit the central throat on **opposite sides** (point-symmetric
about center). In f_0271 one is upper-left ≈(95,70), the other lower-center-right
≈(360,180); a thin pale waveform line still connects them through the throat. They
drag **comet trails** — short fat blurred streaks of gold/violet (the bokeh-like
blobs at f_0269, f_0271, f_0277). f_0277 (t=138) shows the Orbiters near top
swinging, trails curving — they are orbiting the vanishing point.

**Motion / camera.** Strong sense of **camera flying into / orbiting around the
tunnel throat.** The radial fan rotates slowly and the throat drifts, giving an
orbiting-camera read. f_0267–f_0269 the fan tilts (lines fan more to one side);
f_0275 the throat is centered and symmetric (a clean double-cone X of lines);
f_0279 (t=139) the net contracts and a pink feathery tuft re-forms at center
(handing off to G4).

**Feedback.** Still heavy but now **radial** rather than purely diagonal — trails
smear along the radial lines toward/away from the throat. Orbiter blobs leave fat
soft comet smears. Magenta/violet glow pools in the throat (f_0273).

**Color.** Net = cool green/teal/grey. Throat glow = magenta/violet/gold. Orbiters
= red-ringed magenta. The magenta/green opposition is at its clearest here (green
structure vs magenta accents).

**Symmetry.** Strong **point/rotational symmetry** about the throat (the radial fan
and the opposed Orbiter pair). This is the most symmetric scene of the segment.

**Audio hypotheses.** Number/brightness of visible radial lines ∝ energy; tunnel
"zoom" rate ∝ bass. Orbiter orbital angular velocity beat-locked. Connecting
waveform line jaggedness = live samples (low here → nearly straight).

## Scene G4 — Anemone reblooms, then the giant Orbiter X (t≈139.5–148.0, f_0280–f_0297)

**Morph.** From the contracting net, a **feathery Anemone reblooms at center**

**Anemone fully blooms (f_0285–f_0289).** It grows to fill most of the frame as a

**Background.** Dark maroon haze, slightly brighter/redder during the bloom peak

**Motion / camera.** Bloom expands explosively (142→144) then the X rotates and the

**Feedback.** Heavy multidirectional: the burst filaments smear radially; the X

**Color.** Magenta + green duotone at maximum saturation here (the burst alternates

**Symmetry.** Radial (the burst) + the 2-fold/4-fold X of the crossing beams.

**Audio hypotheses.** Burst radius ∝ bass envelope (huge on the loud passage 142–

## Implementation direction for a Butterchurn author (whole segment)

**Coordinate system.** Work in Butterchurn's 0..1 wave space, 0.5 = center. Define
a per-frame **ribbon axis angle `q1`** that slowly rotates (G1 steep diagonal → G2
shallow → G3 radial/none → G4 X) and a **center point `q2,q3`** (the anemone/throat
center) that drifts. Use `q4` = a 0..1 scene phase you advance over time to morph
between the three forms. Drive everything off the same live audio `t.bass_att`
(slow undulation/bloom radius) and the raw waveform samples (jaggedness).

**The Orbiters (use the project's "Dance"/`waveLine` pattern).** This is the spine
of the whole segment — implement first. Two endpoint positions `A`, `B` orbiting
about center (G1/G2: opposite diagonal corners; G3: opposite sides of throat,
point-symmetric; G4: four ends of an X = two waveLines). For each, a `waveLine()`
custom wave whose `point_eqs` marches `a.sample` 0→1 from A to B and displaces
**perpendicular to the A→B axis** by the live sample: `disp = amp*(a.value1-0.5)`
with `amp` scaled by `t.treb_att`. Color the line magenta with additive glow; the
heavy feedback will manufacture the rainbow. Put a small bright disc (a tiny
`circleWave` or a shader dot) at each endpoint with a red halo ring (G3).

**The central cluster (Anemone ↔ Ribbon ↔ Net).** One radial element morphed by
`q4`:
- Anemone/Starburst (G1, G4): many fine radial filaments from center — a custom
  wave with `point_eqs` placing N samples at `r = base + amp*(a.value-0.5)` along
  angle `theta = a.sample*2*PI`, additive, fine. Filament length pulses on bass.
- Ribbon (G2): a near-horizontal `waveLine` (or low-frequency `circleWave`
  flattened) tilted by `q1`, thick and additive, with a raised hump = a gaussian
  bump added to `a.y` near `a.sample≈0.5`, topped by a small bright flare sprite.
- Wireframe-Net (G3): emulate radial straight lines via the **comp shader** — a
  polar grid `sin(ang*Nspokes)` masked to thin lines, converging on `q2,q3`, with a
  dark elliptical throat (radial falloff). Rotate spokes slowly.

**Directional feedback (the dominant look).** This is non-negotiable — most of the
texture is feedback. Set `decay` ≈ 0.96 (G1/G2/G4) and push a directional drift:
`dx/dy` along the ribbon axis `q1` (down-left in G2) for the diagonal streaks; in
G3 switch to a **radial** pull (`zoom` < 1 toward `q2,q3`, small `rot`) for the
tunnel. Keep `wave_smoothing` low so the waveform stays jagged before smearing. The
"combed/venetian-blind" texture is the natural result of a moving high-frequency
line under high decay — don't fake it.

**Color approach.** Two-color **magenta ↔ green** duotone for all geometry; do NOT
use a full rainbow palette directly — let the feedback (and a faint
chromatic/echo offset, `echo_alpha` small) generate the secondary rainbow inside
the trails. Background: near-black with a faint deep-maroon top-center wash (a dim
radial in the comp shader). Per the project muted-Alchemy rule, finish with a
Reinhard tone-map `c/(c+k)` so the bloom peaks (f_0287, f_0291) read as soft glow,
not flat white. Slow hue mix `0.5+0.5*sin(time*0.05)` between magenta and green for
the central cluster matches the observed cycling.

**Scene scheduling.** ~4 s anemone+launch → ~7 s ribbon+weave → ~7 s net/tunnel →
~9 s rebloom+giant-X, all cross-faded via `q4`, no hard cuts. The giant crossing
**X of two live-waveform sawtooth beams** (f_0291) is the signature payoff frame —
make that read clearly.
