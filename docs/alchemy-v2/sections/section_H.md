# Segment H — 2:28–2:48

Frames analysed: f_0297 (t=148.0s) through f_0337 (t=168.0s), 2 fps, 640x360, every 0.5s.
This segment is the long "Wireframe Net / waveform terrain" passage of WMP Alchemy. It is NOT
the 3D Ribbon and it is NOT the final Supernova — it is the connective tissue between them. The
dominant feature across all 41 frames is **dense live oscilloscope geometry rendered as thin
straight line segments** that morph from a chromatic crossed-waveform "X" (Orbiters) into a
receding stacked-waveform net, into a sparse radiating fan of individual strands, and finally
re-condense into a thick green net heading into the next section. I read three sub-scenes plus
two morph transitions; all transitions are continuous morphs driven by frame-feedback, never hard
cuts. Throughout, the look is far more **vivid/chromatic** than the muted Ambience/Battery families
— Alchemy here runs a full rainbow with strong additive glow and heavy horizontal motion-blur
smear, so do NOT force the "muted colors" rule on this preset.

## Sub-scene H1 — Chromatic Crossed-Waveform "X" / Orbiters (t=148.0s–151.5s, f_0297–f_0304)

**Background.** Near-pure black field. There is faint horizontal banding at the very bottom third —
a low, dim band of smeared color (the reflection/feedback wash of the bright geometry above it),
but it does not read as a populated terrain yet; it reads as a black void with a single bright
structure floating in it and a soft mirror beneath. No stars, no tiles.

**Foreground elements & counts.** TWO dominant bright waveform lines that cross near screen center,
forming a shallow **X / scissor** shape. In f_0297 the two lines are nearly vertical and close
together (a narrow inverted-V apex top-center, ~x=0.6, with two legs splaying down to bottom-left
and bottom-right). By f_0298–f_0300 the X has opened up: one line runs upper-left→lower-right, the
other upper-right→lower-left, scissoring through a central crossing point that sits slightly
right-of-center and drifts toward true center. These are the **Orbiters** motif: each line is a real
audio waveform (dense jagged/zigzag micro-displacement along its length — clearly the live
time-domain samples, not a synthetic sine), and each terminates in a brighter blob/knot at its far
end (the "glowing point" trailing a jagged line). Count: 2 primary lines + their mirror reflections
below = visually 4 strokes.

**Geometry & chromatic dispersion.** The signature look here is **prism dispersion**: each waveform
line is not one color but a stacked rainbow ribbon — red/orange on one edge, through green, to
blue/violet on the other, like the line has been split through a prism and the spectral copies are
offset perpendicular to the line. Near the central crossing the dispersion fans out widest (a full
rainbow lens-flare bloom radiating from the crossing point, f_0301). This is the strongest
audio-reactive bloom point.

**Motion.** The two lines rotate/scissor about the central crossing — the apex angle opens from
~25° (f_0297) to ~90°+ (f_0300) over ~1.5s, i.e. the lines sweep outward like opening blades. There
is strong **horizontal smear** on everything (frame-feedback with a horizontal stretch), giving each
bright knot a comet-tail to the left and right. The crossing point itself stays roughly centered
vertically and drifts.

**Camera/perspective.** Low/flat — no strong 3D recession yet; this reads as a 2D plane viewed
head-on with a mirror line slightly below vertical center. The mirror (reflection of the X in a dark
floor) is present from f_0297.

**Feedback / motion blur.** Heavy. Decay is long (~0.95+): previous-frame copies of the lines
linger as the soft horizontal color bands across the lower-middle. Vertical smear is minor;
horizontal smear dominates.

**Color & hue.** Full rainbow via prism dispersion; the dispersion direction cycles so the dominant
tint shifts (magenta/violet heavy in f_0297, greens emerging by f_0302). Additive blending — the
crossing bloom blows toward white-pink.

**Symmetry.** Bilateral about the central crossing + a horizontal mirror (floor reflection).

**Audio hypotheses.** Bass → apex angle / scissor opening speed and the central bloom radius. Treble →
the jaggedness amplitude of the waveform displacement along each line. The terminal knots brighten
on transient hits.

## Morph A (t=151.5s–153.0s, f_0304–f_0307)

The two thick dispersed lines **multiply and tilt into depth**. Between f_0304 and f_0306 the single
crossed pair becomes a *fan of many parallel thin lines* radiating from the crossing — the X grows a
"feathered" set of intermediate strands, and the whole structure tips so it recedes toward a vanishing
crossing near center. By f_0306 two clear **Orbiter blobs** appear at opposite corners (bright white
ovoid at upper-left ~x0.1,y0.35 and lower-right ~x0.85,y0.75), each tethered to the central mesh by a
single thin jagged waveform thread — the canonical Orbiter pair. This is a continuous morph, not a cut.

## Sub-scene H2 — Receding Stacked-Waveform Net / "Terrain Sheet" (t=153.0s–157.5s, f_0307–f_0316)

**Background & whether it reads as 3D terrain.** YES — this is the segment's "waveform terrain"
moment. The screen is filled by a **dense sheet of many stacked, near-parallel horizontal lines**
that bow and twist like a sheet of fabric/flag seen at a low oblique angle. It genuinely reads as a
3D plane of stacked waveforms: the lines are closely spaced and converge toward the left/center
(perspective compression), and spread/coarsen toward the right (nearer to camera), giving the
classic topographic "receding ribbon of scanlines" feel. The lower third carries a smeared color
reflection of the sheet (magenta/red/green horizontal bands), reinforcing the "plane over a
reflective floor" read.

**Foreground elements.** ONE large coherent mesh-sheet (hundreds of thin lines) that occupies most
of the frame, plus the 2 Orbiter blobs at corners early in the sub-scene (f_0306–f_0309) that fade as
the sheet densifies. The sheet has a distinct **dense vertical-stripe cross-hatch** texture — it is
actually two families of lines: a set running along the sheet (the waveforms) and a denser set of
short perpendicular ticks, producing a woven/window-screen moiré (very visible f_0307–f_0309,
f_0316).

**Geometry & motion.** The sheet **twists and sweeps**: in f_0307 it is a near-vertical bowed wall
tilting left; by f_0310–f_0312 it has rotated to a strong upper-left→lower-right diagonal drape with
the lines fanning from a tight node on the left edge out toward the lower-right (a "comet of strands").
Continuous left-to-right sweep + rotation about a left-side hub. The waveform jaggedness is present
but subtler here than in H1 because the lines are thin and densely packed (the moiré of the live
samples reads as shimmer/noise across the sheet).

**Camera/perspective.** Low-angle 3D over a plane, definitely. Lines converge to the left hub
(vanishing region) and expand to the right — perspective recession is the defining cue.

**Feedback / blur.** Long decay produces horizontal banded ghosting in the lower half; the bright
green dominates because green sits at the additive peak of the palette here (f_0307–f_0310 are
strongly green/yellow-green).

**Color & hue.** Shifts from rainbow (f_0307 still multi-colored) to **green/yellow-green dominant**
(f_0308–f_0312), with magenta/red surviving only in the reflection band. Then begins drifting toward
amber/orange (f_0312–f_0313) and lavender/pink (f_0314–f_0316). So hue is cycling slowly across the
whole sub-scene (green → amber → violet over ~4s).

**Symmetry.** Approximate top/bottom mirror (floor reflection) persists; left-right is asymmetric
(hub on the left).

**Audio hypotheses.** Each stacked line = one historical frame of the waveform scrolled in depth
(a waterfall/terrain of time-domain history). Bass → overall sheet twist/sweep speed and the
brightness pulse. Treble → per-line jagged amplitude (the shimmer/moiré density).

## Sub-scene H3 — Sparse Thin-Line Wireframe Fan / Net (t=157.5s–163.5s, f_0316–f_0328)

**Background.** Returns to near-black with only a faint warm (amber/olive) horizontal glow band on
the right and lower edges — the terrain wash recedes, leaving the void. This is the cleanest,
darkest part of the segment.

**Foreground elements & geometry.** The dense sheet **thins out into individual, clearly resolvable
straight line segments** — a sparse **Wireframe Net / fan**. Tens (not hundreds) of long straight
strands radiate from a tight hub on the LEFT edge and fan out across to the right, crossing each
other to weave an open net (f_0316–f_0322). The strands are dead-straight chords, not jagged — at
this sparsity the structure reads as a geometric line-art mesh / string-art spirograph rather than an
oscilloscope. By f_0323–f_0328 the hub migrates and the fan re-orients: strands sweep from a
lower-left hub up and to the right, layering new fans over old ones so the net re-densifies.

**Motion.** Slow, elegant sweep of the whole fan, pivoting about the moving hub; new strands are laid
down each frame while old ones decay, so the net appears to "comb" across the frame left-to-right and
rotate. Lower energy / calmer than H1–H2.

**Camera/perspective.** Flatter again — reads as a 2D string-art plane with mild perspective from the
hub. The convergence at the hub still gives a shallow 3D fan.

**Feedback / blur.** Lower decay here (lines are crisp, less smear) — the net looks sharp and
wiry. Some faint ghosting of prior fans remains as the secondary crossing strands.

**Color & hue.** Slow cycle: green/yellow (f_0316, f_0322–f_0323) → teal/blue (f_0319–f_0321) →
violet/magenta (f_0324–f_0327) → magenta+green mix (f_0328). Lines are individually tinted along their
length (a strand fades green→blue→violet end to end), confirming a position/angle-based palette.

**Symmetry.** Largely radial-from-hub; no strong mirror.

**Audio hypotheses.** Number of active strands and fan-opening angle track bass; the hub position
walks with a slow LFO (beat-locked drift). Treble adds slight wobble to strand endpoints.

## Morph B → Sub-scene H4 — Re-condensing Green Net into Terrain (t=163.5s–168.0s, f_0328–f_0337)

From f_0329 onward the sparse fan **re-thickens rapidly back into a dense net/terrain**, previewing
the next section. f_0329–f_0331 show the strands multiplying and bunching into a tight cross-hatched
sheet again (yellow/green with a magenta companion fan), with the woven moiré returning. f_0332–f_0337
go strongly **green-dominant**: a dense scanline sheet sweeping diagonally, the lower half a bright
green reflective terrain wash, and by f_0335–f_0337 the whole frame is saturated green with a
twisting bright diagonal waveform line cutting through it (f_0337 shows a clean green X-crossing of two
bright waveform strokes over a green field — visually echoing H1 but in mono-green and far denser).
This is the hand-off to the following segment; energy and density are ramping up.

## Implementation direction (Butterchurn author)

**Coordinate system.** Center origin, 0.5 = screen center. Use a left-side hub variable `hx,hy`
(walked by a slow LFO, `q1=0.15+0.1*sin(time*0.2)`) as the common anchor for fans/sheets.

**Stacked / receding waveform terrain (H2, H4).** Render with **N custom waves** (e.g. 24–48 enabled
waveLine waves), one per stacked line, each offset in a "depth" parameter `k=i/N` (0..1). Per wave:
- baseline y = `hy + (k-0.5)*spread`, and apply perspective compression toward the hub: x runs from
  `hx` out to `hx + (1-k*0.6)*reach` so far lines are shorter/converged.
- each point: `a.y += dispAmt*(a.value1-0.5)*persp` where `persp = mix(0.2,1.0,k)` (near lines wave
  more) — this is the live time-domain jaggedness; `dispAmt` scales with `treb_att`.
- color along depth: tint by `k` through the cycling palette (`pal(k + time*0.05)`).
This gives the "waterfall of stacked live waveforms receding to a hub" terrain read.

**Crossed Orbiters (H1) & sparse fan (H3).** Use 2 primary `waveLine` waves marching A→B between two
orbiter endpoints that orbit opposite corners (reuse the project's Dance/Alchemy-Random orbiter
pattern), with perpendicular `a.value`-driven displacement for the jagged line. For the sparse net
(H3) add ~16 additional straight waveLines from the moving hub at fanned angles `ang=base+i*da`, only
lightly displaced (these read as straight string-art).

**Chromatic prism dispersion (H1).** Draw each primary wave 3–5 times with a small perpendicular
pixel offset per color channel (R offset one way, B the other) in additive mode — cheap prism split.
Alternatively do the RGB-split in the `comp` shader by sampling `sampler_main` at `uv ± chroma` per
channel, `chroma` scaled by `bass`.

**Feedback / blur.** `decay ≈ 0.94–0.96`, `additivewave = 1`, plus a **horizontal smear**: in warp
shader sample a few taps along +x/−x and blend (or set `dx` small + `wrap=0`) to get the comet trails.
H3 wants lower decay (~0.9, crisper); modulate decay by sub-scene/time.

**Floor reflection.** Add a mirrored copy: in comp shader blend `texture2D(sampler_main, vec2(uv.x,
1.0-uv.y))*0.4` into the lower band, or render each wave twice mirrored about `y=0.42`.

**Color approach.** Full rainbow, NOT muted. Slow global hue cycle `hue=time*0.05`; bias toward green
in H2/H4 (`+0.33`), toward magenta/violet in mid-H3. Strong additive glow + a gentle Reinhard
tone-map only to keep the brightest crossing blooms from hard-clipping to flat white.

**Audio mapping.** bass/bass_att → sweep speed, fan angle, crossing-bloom radius, sheet twist;
treb_att → per-line jagged amplitude and prism `chroma` width; mid → hub LFO gain.
