# Segment I — 2:48–3:06 (finale)

Frames f_0337–f_0373 (168.0s–186.0s, 2 fps, 640x360). This is the closing
segment of the Alchemy reference video. It contains **three** distinct scenes,
not two, plus a hard end-of-clip freeze. The "Supernova" the brief predicted is
present (the violently radiating Anemone/Starburst, f_0352–f_0362), but the
*finale* image is NOT a zoom-to-center eye/tunnel that keeps accelerating — the
clip ends on a **frozen perspective corridor** (an X-shaped wireframe tunnel with
a small twinkling node at the vanishing point) because **playback was paused**:
the WMP transport bar appears at f_0370 and the picture is byte-identical from
f_0367 through f_0373. So the visualization does not gracefully "end"; the
recording stops on a held frame. Document accordingly: the corridor is the last
*live* state, and an implementation should treat it as a steady scene, not a
fade-out.

Scene map for this segment:
- **Scene I-A — Orbiters over a Wireframe-Net dome (168.0s–175.0s, f_0337–f_0351).**
  The familiar two-orbiter motif (two glowing pucks trailing jagged live-audio
  lines) sitting on a dark dome wrapped in a fine concentric wire-net, on a
  green-wash floor. This is the *carry-in* from the previous segment, decaying.
- **Scene I-B — The Supernova / Anemone (175.5s–181.0s, f_0352–f_0362).** A single
  central radiating sea-urchin of dense fine live-audio filaments, pulsing and
  hue-cycling violently (blue→pink→yellow→green), flanked by two long lightning
  lines exiting to opposite corners. This is the climax.
- **Scene I-C — The Perspective Tunnel / Corridor (181.5s–186.0s, f_0363–f_0373).**
  The anemone collapses; the two lightning lines straighten into a green
  wireframe ground-plane corridor receding to a center vanishing point, with a
  small yellow/orange twinkling node where the anemone used to be. Freezes here.

Transitions are **morphs**, not cuts: I-A→I-B (f_0351→f_0352) is a fast
contraction of the two orbiters into one merged central cluster (~0.5s, reads
almost as a snap but the green net lines persist through it); I-B→I-C
(f_0362→f_0363) is a continuous flattening where the radial filaments lie down
into the receding ground plane over ~1.5s.

## Scene I-A — Orbiters over the Wireframe-Net dome (168.0s–175.0s)

**Background.** Near-black with a strong **green** cast. The lower third is a
luminous lime/emerald floor-wash that brightens on the beat; the upper area is
black. In the earliest frames (f_0337–f_0339) the green is a broad diagonal
sheet of fine parallel lines (a previous-segment fan still dissipating) crossing
the frame top-left to bottom-right. By f_0340 this resolves into the structured
scene below. There is a small persistent dim orange/red speck top-right in
f_0337 (a leftover hotspot), gone by f_0339.

**Foreground elements & counts.** TWO orbiters — the WMP signature. Each is a
flattened glowing disc/puck (think a poker chip seen at a low angle): a bright
core ringed by a darker torus and an outer colored halo. They start small and
high-contrast and **bloom** through the scene: at f_0341–f_0342 they're small
amber/green coins with thin halos; by f_0347–f_0348 they have swollen into large
soft pink-cored discs with red rims; by f_0349–f_0350 they are big blue-cored
discs with magenta under-shadows; f_0351 shows them as bright cyan-white
cores. The two orbiters sit close together, lower-center, the left one slightly
higher than the right, gently see-sawing (a slow scissoring orbit about their
shared midpoint). Below each orbiter is a soft colored reflection/bloom on the
green floor (pink/red in f_0347–0348, multicolor smear in f_0350).

**The Wireframe Net.** This is the defining structure of I-A. A **dome of fine
concentric arcs** (a hemispheric wire-mesh) wraps the upper-back of the scene,
radiating from behind the orbiters. In f_0338–f_0339 it reads as a near-symmetric
radial spray of fine cyan/green lines emanating from a center high in the frame
(an almost mandala/peacock-tail burst). As the scene settles (f_0343 onward) the
net becomes a clean **horizontally-banded hemisphere**: dozens of evenly-spaced
concentric ribs of pale green/cyan dots-and-dashes arcing left-to-right over the
top, like the inside of a wire colander or a radar dome. The ribs are densest and
brightest at f_0343–f_0347, then thin out and dim toward f_0350–f_0351 as the
scene begins collapsing into I-B. The net is clearly built from **fine live-audio
lines** (the rib spacing flickers/jitters with the audio), not smooth vector
arcs.

**Geometry & camera.** 3D, low camera angle looking slightly down at a tilted
ground plane, the net forming a dome overhead/behind. The orbiters sit on the
plane. There is gentle camera drift — the dome's apparent center wanders and the
whole structure rolls a few degrees — giving a "floating through a domed space"
feel rather than a locked shot.

**Motion.** Slow and lyrical compared with what follows. The two orbiters
pulse-bloom on the beat (cores flare, halos expand), scissor slowly, and the dome
ribs shimmer. No violent expansion yet — this is the calm before the Supernova.

**Motion blur / feedback.** Moderate frame-feedback: orbiter blooms leave soft
ghosts, the floor-wash smears. Decay is medium (trails last ~0.3–0.5s).

**Waveforms.** The dome ribs and the faint diagonal lightning lines are real
live-audio oscilloscope lines — dense, fine, jittery.

**Color & hue cycling.** Dominant green/cyan structure with the orbiter cores
sweeping through a hue cycle over the scene: amber→pink/red→blue→cyan-white
(f_0341→f_0351). High contrast against black. Saturated, NOT muted here (this
preset's finale is genuinely vivid — match the reference, don't force pastels).

**Symmetry.** Strong left-right (bilateral) symmetry of the dome; the two
orbiters are a near-mirrored pair about the vertical center.

**Audio-reactivity hypotheses.** Orbiter core brightness + halo radius = bass
envelope. Dome rib jitter/density = treble/mid (the fine line detail). Floor-wash
brightness = overall level. The scissoring orbit is a slow LFO independent of
audio.

## Scene I-B — The Supernova / Anemone (175.5s–181.0s)

This is the climax and the most important scene to nail.

**Background.** Pure black except for the central object and two thin lightning
lines that shoot to opposite corners. A faint horizontal green smear sits behind
the urchin (residual floor-wash) in f_0352–f_0355, fading to full black by
f_0358.

**Foreground element.** ONE central **Anemone/Starburst** — a roughly circular
sea-urchin of **hundreds of fine radial filaments** emanating from a small dark
central pupil. It fills ~50–65% of the frame height, centered. The filaments are
fine live-audio lines (each radial spoke wiggles/jags with the waveform), giving a
furry, "blown dandelion / sea-urchin" texture, NOT clean rays. There is a small
dark **eye/pupil** at the exact center throughout (f_0352–f_0360 show a clear dark
oval hole), reinforcing the "eye" reading.

**Evolution frame-by-frame.**
- f_0352: blue/cyan urchin, tight, dark pupil, modest radius.
- f_0353: grows; rim turns pink/magenta, core stays blue, pink under-bloom on
  floor.
- f_0354: pink/red dominant, fuzzier, radius larger, red core flash.
- f_0355: hue flips to **yellow/gold** filaments with a pink/blue under-halo;
  pupil now a pale lens.
- f_0356: full **yellow** urchin, very dense, with two faint lightning lines
  beginning to extend from the core.
- f_0357: yellow/green, rim picks up rainbow fringing; a faint wire-net dome
  briefly re-ghosts behind it (top corners).
- f_0358: **peak Supernova** — huge multicolor (orange/pink/violet) urchin
  filling the frame, and TWO bright white-cored lightning lines now blast fully
  out to the lower-left and upper-right corners, each ending in a small green
  glowing node (the orbiters, re-emerged as the line endpoints). A small
  cyan/teal core sits dead center.
- f_0359: violet/pink urchin, the two corner nodes are green pucks, lines are
  ragged live-audio zigzags (classic "lightning").
- f_0360: yellow/green urchin, lines now blue/violet, dark central slit.
- f_0361: the urchin starts to **tilt and flatten** — top develops a 5–6 point
  star silhouette (green/yellow), the bottom blooms pink — first sign of
  collapse into the tunnel.
- f_0362: clearly tipping over — a green 5-point star lying back, the lightning
  line now running across the lower third as a near-horizontal ribbon.

**Geometry & camera.** Radial/polar object centered in frame. Through f_0360 the
camera holds roughly head-on; at f_0361–f_0362 the apparent viewpoint pitches so
we look *down the axis* of the urchin — that reframing is what produces the tunnel
in I-C. The two lightning lines act as a single straight axis through the core
(corner to opposite corner), pivoting slowly.

**Motion.** Violent, beat-driven **expansion/contraction** of the urchin radius
(the "Supernova" pulse) plus a rapid hue cycle (one full sweep every ~2–3s here:
blue→pink→yellow→green→violet). The two lightning lines whip/recoil. This is the
energy peak of the whole piece.

**Motion blur / feedback.** Heavier than I-A — each pulse leaves a soft radial
bloom ghost; the under-floor keeps a colored after-smear. Decay still medium
(~0.4s); it does NOT yet ramp into the runaway zoom feedback.

**Waveforms.** Everything is live audio: the radial filaments ARE the waveform
sampled around a circle (per-spoke displacement = sample value); the two corner
lines are a straight oscilloscope with perpendicular sample displacement
(identical technique to "Dance"/"Alchemy Random" waveLine).

**Color & hue cycling.** Full rainbow cycle, high saturation, high contrast on
black. Cyan→pink→gold→green→violet, fast. Vivid (reference-accurate).

**Symmetry.** Radial (rotational) symmetry of the urchin; bilateral symmetry of
the two-corner lightning axis.

**Audio-reactivity hypotheses.** Urchin radius & filament length = bass (violent
pulse on kick). Filament jitter/jaggedness = treble (fine line detail). Hue phase
= slow time LFO. Lightning-line endpoints (green nodes) = the orbiters whose
amplitude tracks bass.

## Scene I-C — Perspective Tunnel / Corridor (181.5s–186.0s) — the held finale

**Background.** Black, with a receding **green wireframe ground/corridor**. The
two lightning lines from I-B have straightened and spread into a giant **X**
across the frame: two broad green planes meeting at a center vanishing point — a
floor plane sweeping up from the bottom and a back wall/ceiling plane, forming a
one-point-perspective tunnel/road receding to center.

**Evolution.**
- f_0363: the X is sharp and bright — four green beams from the four corners
  converging on a small central knot of rainbow color (the dying urchin core).
- f_0364: the X softens; the lower planes fill with green wash, vanishing point
  glows cyan/orange.
- f_0365–f_0366: resolves into the final corridor — a green wire ground plane
  (fine parallel ribs, like a Tron grid receding to center), magenta/pink haze
  along the horizon, and a **small yellow/orange twinkling node** stacked at the
  vanishing point (a little vertical chain of warm dots — the last ember of the
  anemone).
- f_0367–f_0369: **identical** to f_0366 — the picture has stopped changing.
- f_0370: the **WMP transport/seek bar fades in** at the bottom (play/pause,
  timecode, volume) — playback was paused.
- f_0371–f_0373: identical held frame with controls visible.

So I-C is effectively a **static end card**: a green perspective corridor with a
warm twinkling node at the vanishing point. The clip ENDS by **freezing** (paused
playback), NOT by fading to black, NOT by a runaway zoom. Do not implement a
fade; if you want a graceful loop, hold this corridor briefly then cut back to the
opening.

**Geometry/camera.** One-point perspective, vanishing point dead center, camera
looking straight down a tunnel/road. **Color:** green corridor, magenta horizon
band, warm yellow/orange center node — green/pink high-contrast complementary
pair. **Symmetry:** 4-fold X about center; strong radial convergence.
**Audio-reactivity:** in the live (non-frozen) f_0363–0366 the corridor ribs and
center node twinkle with treble; the green plane brightness tracks level.

## Implementation direction (Butterchurn author)

**Coordinate system:** polar/radial for I-A and I-B; perspective-warp for I-C.
Drive all line detail from real samples (`a.value1/value2`).

**Scene I-A (Orbiters + Wire-Net dome).** Two `circleWave(qx,qy)` ringed pucks
positioned via `frame_eqs` q-vars on a slow scissor LFO (`qx = ±0.18*sin(time)`,
`qy` slightly offset). Add the dome as a stack of concentric arcs: either many
`waveLine()` waves with y offset stepped per index and x mapped over a half-arc,
or a comp-shader that draws `sin(rib*pr)` rings masked to the upper hemisphere
with aspect correction (`d.x *= resolution.x/resolution.y`). Green/cyan base,
orbiter cores hue-cycled amber→pink→blue→cyan via `0.5+0.5*sin(time*0.4 + phase)`.
Medium `decay` (~0.95), `additivewave 1`.

**Scene I-B (Supernova/Anemone).** THE key effect: one radial custom wave that is
the live waveform wrapped around a circle with **per-sample radial displacement** —
`a.r0 = baseR + amp*a.value1; a.x = 0.5 + a.r0*cos(theta); a.y = 0.5 +
a.r0*aspect*sin(theta)` where `theta = a.sample*2*PI` and `amp = 0.15 + 0.4*bass`.
Use a high sample count and `wave_smoothing 0` for the furry urchin texture; keep
a small dark center (don't draw the inner radius). Hue-cycle the whole thing fast
with `pal(time*0.5)` (full rainbow, saturated). Add two `waveLine()` lightning
axes from corner to corner through center, endpoints = small green `circleWave`
nodes; pivot the axis slowly (`rot += 0.01`). Bass spikes `amp` for the violent
expansion/contraction. Bloom via `decay ~0.96` + `additivewave`.

**Scene I-C (Tunnel) & ending.** Ramp `frame_eqs` to flatten: increase `zoom`
toward >1 and add `dy` so the radial object lies into a receding plane, OR
crossfade to a dedicated comp shader that draws a one-point-perspective grid
(`uv` mapped so lines converge at center: `gridx = fract(d.x / d.y * k)`),
green ribs, magenta horizon band, warm twinkling node at center scaled by treble.
**Do NOT** build an accelerating infinite-zoom feedback finale — the reference
does not do that; it holds the corridor. End by **holding** the corridor (steady
`decay`, no zoom ramp) — there is no fade in the source.
